on soft rot & spring rituals

somewhere between the moldy fruit and the first bloom of crocus, I found a rhythm. I lit a stick of incense in the attic, scanned old polaroids, and pressed violets into a notebook I'd abandoned in 2019.

the garden grows teeth

the marigolds bite back. petals like bruised knuckles, stems like steel wire. you plant beauty, but it demands blood and fingernails.

ghosts in the laundromat

I saw her reflection in the glass of the spin cycle — a woman with one gold earring and a face like mine. Maybe I imagined it.

where the sidewalk ends

old railway beds and creekside gravel. sunburnt shoulders and the occasional coyote print. a guide to wandering around Indy’s soft green edges.

about indeezine

born out of back alley cafés, midwest thrift bins, and one too many rainy sundays, indeezine is a soft-grit love letter to Indianapolis and the stories that live in its wallpaper.
